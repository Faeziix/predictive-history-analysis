# Trump Tariff Regime — Largest Tax Increase Since 1993, Reshoring Push

## Summary
Trump has implemented the largest tariff increases as % of GDP since 1993 ($1,500/household). IEEPA tariffs struck down by Supreme Court, replaced with Section 122 surcharges. DOGE dismantled ~9% of federal workforce. Industrial production up, steel surpassed Japan. But mass reshoring remains "distant reality." This supports both the "Technate" and "regional mercantilism" theses.

## Key Quotes
> "The largest US tax increase as a percent of GDP since 1993"
> "For the first time in over 25 years, the United States surpassed Japan in crude steel production"
> "Progress on reshoring has been lackluster"
> DOGE: ~212,000 federal employees cut (~9% of civilian workforce)

## Source Credibility Assessment
Tax Foundation is nonpartisan and authoritative on fiscal matters. USTR and marketplace.org provide official and balanced reporting.
