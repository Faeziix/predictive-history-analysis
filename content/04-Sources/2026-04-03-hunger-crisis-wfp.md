# Middle East War Risks Pushing 45 Million More People into Acute Hunger

## Summary
The World Food Programme warns the Middle East conflict could push 45 million additional people into acute hunger by mid-2026. Already 318 million people face crisis-level hunger globally, with 673 million chronically hungry.

## Key Data Points
- 318 million people face crisis-level hunger or worse in 2026
- 673 million chronically hungry (8.2% of world population)
- Iran war could push 45 million more into acute hunger by mid-2026
- Two simultaneous famines confirmed in Gaza and Sudan in 2025 (first this century)
- 87 million facing hunger in East/Southern Africa
- 52 million projected food insecure in West/Central Africa by mid-2026
- 69% of food crises directly caused by war
- Sudan: 10 million internally displaced

## Bias Assessment
UN agencies are authoritative sources on humanitarian data. High credibility for food security statistics and projections.
