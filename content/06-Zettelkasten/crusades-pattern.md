# Crusades Pattern

When eschatological motivation meets geopolitical ambition, with Jerusalem as the prize. The Crusades demonstrate how religious narrative mobilizes continental-scale military operations and creates civilizational fault lines that persist for centuries.

## The Framework Lesson

The Crusades are a historical case of the [[law-of-eschatological-convergence]]:
- Christian kingdoms mobilized across Europe for Jerusalem
- Islamic powers unified to resist (Saladin's counter-crusade)
- Religious motivation produced commitment and coordination beyond what secular politics could generate
- The conflict reshaped the Middle East for centuries

## Modern Parallel

Jiang frames the current convergence on Jerusalem as a new Crusade:
- [[christian-zionist-eschatology]] provides the Western religious motivation
- [[islamic-eschatology]] provides the Islamic defensive motivation
- [[jewish-eschatology]] provides the local acceleration
- The [[third-temple-prophecy]] is the new Holy Sepulchre - the sacred site worth fighting for
