# Christian Zionist Eschatology

The end-times framework within American evangelical Christianity that drives US foreign policy support for Israel - not for geopolitical reasons, but for theological ones.

## Key Elements

- **Third Temple as prerequisite.** The [[third-temple-prophecy|Third Temple]] must be rebuilt before Jesus returns
- **Battle of Armageddon.** A final battle in the Holy Land precedes the Second Coming
- **The Rapture.** True believers are taken up to heaven before (or during) the tribulation
- **Israel's role.** The existence and expansion of Israel is fulfillment of biblical prophecy

## Political Significance

Christian Zionism is one of the largest voting blocs in American politics:

- Core of Trump's evangelical base
- Provides bipartisan congressional support for Israel
- Translates into billions in US military aid to Israel
- Creates political pressure *against* any peace deal that constrains Israeli expansion

The critical insight for the [[law-of-eschatological-convergence]]: these voters support Israel not because they love Jewish people but because they need Israel to fulfill *Christian* prophecy. The Third Temple triggers the Second Coming. Israeli expansion is a means to a Christian end.

## Why It Creates Self-Fulfilling Prophecy

When the most powerful nation on earth (the US) shapes its Middle East policy around eschatological beliefs:
- Military support for Israel becomes unconditional
- Palestinian/Arab interests are structurally deprioritized
- Escalation toward "the final battle" is *incentivized* by the theology
- Leaders who would restrain Israel face political destruction from the evangelical base

## Convergence

Christian Zionist eschatology converges with [[jewish-eschatology]] on the Third Temple but *diverges* on the endpoint: Christians believe Jews must convert at the Second Coming. This creates a temporary alliance with a built-in future conflict.
