# Wall Street (The Old Elite)

In the [[elite-overproduction]] framework, Wall Street represents the establishment faction - the "old elite" that has controlled the American state through financial extraction for decades, now facing an existential challenge from the [[silicon-valley-actor|counter-elite]].

## The Faction

- **Political alignment:** Democrats, legacy institutions
- **Economic base:** Finance, private credit, media, academia
- **Key institutions:** Major banks, hedge funds, legacy media (NYT, CNN)
- **Financial interest:** Government bailouts for the $2 trillion private credit bubble

## What They Want

1. **Maintain the empire** - The global financial extraction system benefits them
2. **Iran war as weapon** - Want the war to be a catastrophe they can blame on Trump
3. **2028 victory** - Use the disaster to recapture the presidency
4. **Bailout** - Secure government backing for their financial positions before the crash

## The Losing Position

Jiang suggests the old elite is fighting a losing battle:
- The [[petrodollar-system]] they depend on is collapsing
- Trump is deliberately purging their institutional positions
- The Epstein files ([[CLAIM-013-epstein-intel-networks]]) are being used to destroy their authority
- Their financial bubble will pop alongside the counter-elite's, but they have less political protection

## The Epstein Dimension

The Substack article "Twilight of the Liberal Elite" frames the Epstein file release as a strategic weapon:
- Released in batches, each more explosive
- Targets the liberal establishment (academics, politicians, media figures)
- Destroys "elite authority" (Hannah Arendt's framework)
- Paves the way for autocratic governance by discrediting democratic institutions
