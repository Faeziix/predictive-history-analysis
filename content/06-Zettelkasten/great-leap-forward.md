# Great Leap Forward

What happens when ideology overrides practical governance. Mao's campaign (1958-1962) caused the largest famine in human history (~30-45 million dead) through centralized ideological mandates that ignored physical reality.

## The Framework Lesson

The Great Leap Forward demonstrates the extreme consequences of [[platos-cave-analogy|living in the cave]]:
- Officials reported false harvest numbers to match ideological targets
- The "shadows" (propaganda) became more important than reality (crop yields)
- Anyone who challenged the narrative was purged
- Tens of millions died because the system couldn't process truth

## Connection to Depopulation

Jiang references this as a historical precedent for [[CLAIM-015-depopulation-resource-wars]]: when systems that sustain large populations collapse (whether from ideology or from [[oil-fertilizer-food-chain|energy disruption]]), the depopulation that follows is measured in tens of millions.
