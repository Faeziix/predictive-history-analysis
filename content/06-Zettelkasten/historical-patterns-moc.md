# Historical Patterns

Professor Jiang grounds his predictions in historical precedent. These aren't just illustrations - they're the evidence base for his "laws." Each pattern demonstrates a framework principle playing out in a real context.

## Empire and Decline
- [[fall-of-roman-republic]] - Elite overproduction, civil war, transition from republic to empire. The template for American decline.
- [[spanish-empire-decline]] - How dependence on extracted wealth (gold/silver) weakened domestic production. Parallel to US dependence on financial extraction.
- [[british-empire-decline]] - How overextension and two world wars ended Pax Britannica. Template for how Pax Americana ends.
- [[ottoman-empire-decline]] - The "sick man" phenomenon. How empires die slowly then suddenly.

## Asymmetric Warfare
- [[greeks-vs-persians]] - The original asymmetry. A smaller, more cohesive force defeating a larger empire.
- [[vietnam-war-pattern]] - The modern template for [[law-of-asymmetry]]. Technological superiority defeated by commitment and terrain.
- [[afghanistan-graveyard-of-empires]] - Soviet and American failures demonstrating the same pattern.
- [[us-iraq-war-lessons]] - The immediate precedent for a US-Iran war and its likely outcome.

## Financial Manipulation
- [[2008-financial-crisis-pattern]] - The modern template: predatory lending, bailouts for institutions, losses for citizens.
- The 1929 crash and earlier speculative manias (Tulip Mania, South Sea Bubble) follow the same [[engineered-boom-bust-cycles]] pattern.

## Religious Conflict
- [[crusades-pattern]] - When eschatological motivation meets geopolitical ambition. Jerusalem as the prize.
- [[fall-of-constantinople]] - The Second Rome falls, setting up Russia's claim as [[third-rome-concept|Third Rome]].
- [[great-schism-pattern]] - How religious splits create civilizational fault lines that persist for centuries.

## Revolution and Social Collapse
- [[great-leap-forward]] - What happens when ideology overrides practical governance. Famine and depopulation.
- [[french-revolution-pattern]] - [[elite-overproduction]] in action. Too many elites, not enough positions, systemic collapse.
- [[weimar-republic-pattern]] - Economic humiliation producing radical politics. Parallel to post-2008 populism.

## How to Read These

Each historical pattern note explains: (1) what happened, (2) which framework it demonstrates, and (3) what parallel Jiang draws to today. The patterns aren't perfect analogies - they're structural rhymes.
