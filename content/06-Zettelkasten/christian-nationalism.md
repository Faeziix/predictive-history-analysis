# Christian Nationalism

The ideological foundation for the [[technate]]. Not traditional Christianity applied to politics, but a specific fusion of religious identity, national renewal, and resistance to the secular global order.

## In the Framework

Christian Nationalism serves as the *cohesion mechanism* for post-empire America. Per the [[social-contract-vs-iron-and-blood]] analysis, the American "Nation Game" (meritocratic, transactional) is collapsing because the game is visibly rigged. Christian Nationalism provides a replacement basis for social cohesion:

- **Identity:** "We are a Christian nation" replaces "we are a nation of immigrants"
- **Purpose:** Building the Technate replaces maintaining the empire
- **Enemy:** Secular globalism / [[transnational-capital]] replaces terrorism
- **Cohesion:** Religious duty replaces transactional self-interest

## Key Actors

- **Trump** - Uses Christian Nationalism as political fuel
- **JD Vance** - The "technetronic president" who bridges tech and faith
- **Peter Hegseth** - Freemasonic/evangelical apocalyptic eschatology, "warrior ethos," wants to rebuild Solomon's Temple
- **Peter Thiel** - Argued liberalism must yield to theocracy
- **Evangelical base** - [[christian-zionist-eschatology]] overlaps with domestic nationalism

## Connection to Eschatology

Christian Nationalism intersects with [[christian-zionist-eschatology]] but isn't identical:
- **Christian Zionism:** Supports Israel for theological reasons (Third Temple, Second Coming)
- **Christian Nationalism:** Prioritizes American renewal, may eventually conflict with Israeli interests

This tension is built into the [[technate]] vs [[pax-judaica-concept]] dynamic. The eschatological alliance between American evangelicals and Israel is temporary - once America retreats from global empire, the domestic focus of Christian Nationalism diverges from Israel's global ambitions.
