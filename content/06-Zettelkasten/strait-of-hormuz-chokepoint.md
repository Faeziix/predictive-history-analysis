# Strait of Hormuz Chokepoint

**Core idea:** The narrow waterway between Iran and Oman through which ~20% of the world's oil passes. Iran's ability to close it is the single most consequential asymmetric weapon on the planet - it costs almost nothing to deploy and costs the global economy trillions.

## Why It Matters

The Strait is the physical bottleneck that makes the entire [[price-hierarchy]] vulnerable:

- ~20% of global oil transits through it daily
- Japan, China, India, and Europe depend on this flow for survival
- The GCC states export almost all their oil through it
- There is no alternative route at comparable volume

Closing the Strait triggers the [[price-hierarchy]] cascade: oil price spike -> fertilizer collapse -> food crisis -> economic implosion. This is the [[oil-fertilizer-food-chain]] in action.

## Iran's Weapon

Iran doesn't need to win a naval battle. It needs to make the Strait unusable:
- Sea mines (cheap, effective, hard to clear)
- Anti-ship missiles from Iranian coastline
- Fast attack boats
- Drone swarms
- Submarine operations

The [[cost-pyramid]] math: Iran can threaten the Strait with millions in cheap weapons. Protecting it requires billions in naval assets that are themselves vulnerable.

## Strategic Flexibility

Jiang highlights Iran's ability to *selectively* close the strait:
- Close it to US-allied shipping but allow Chinese or Russian vessels through
- Impose a "toll" rather than a total blockade
- Calibrate pressure to achieve political objectives

This is the [[law-of-escalation]] applied to maritime chokepoints. Iran has more options (gradations of closure) than the US has responses (protect everything or nothing).
